Doodle Jump is a jumping platform game that allows a "doodler" to jump through a never-ending series of platforms and avoid falling. It was released in 2009 from the studio Lima Sky, and had great a reception. On phones, players play the game by tilting their phone side to side to horizontally navigate the endlessly scrolling world. There are also obstacles to avoid, like black holes, monsters, and UFOS. The original doodler is able to shoot and eliminate monsters.  There are also power-ups, like jetpacks, springs, propeller hats, and invincibility shields. At higher levels, there are higher incidences of obstacles. There are also moving platforms and broken platforms, which crumble once they've been jumped on once.

## Our Demake!

In the absence of tilting a phone, players navigate our platformed world using their left and right buttons. The original Doodle Jump had horizontal screen wrapping to allow the player to jump from one side of the screen to the next, so in our version we did not set horiontal bounds to allow for the metasprite to wrap as well. We also had the idea of "broken blocks," which break once they are jumped on. 

Blocks are spawn randomly on the y-axis, with a probability of being drawn on each row. Once rows are passed, they are regenerated. The probability that the broken blocks spawn increases as the game progresses, in line with the original game which gets harder the "higher" you get. Collision detection is performed by checking each row and seeing if there is a platform drawn as a certain x-position. Since there are only 60 blocks, this is preferable to checking the vram which is constantly being updated.

Some other things that the original game had were enemies, a score input at the top, and multiple items. In our version, we did not implement enemies and decided to print the score at the end once the player falls. We did however add a star item that gives bonus points if the player picks it up by jumping on it.
